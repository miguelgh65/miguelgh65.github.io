Projects at Politecnico di Milano

Time Series Predictor

Within the Master's in Computer Science at the Politecnico di Milano, I developed an advanced model for time series prediction. The primary goal of this project was to explore and improve the accuracy in predicting such series.

To achieve precise prediction, we opted for the Transformers architecture. This approach has proven to be particularly effective for various artificial intelligence tasks, including time series.

The model was implemented in Python, and we used PyTorch for its construction and training. To visualize and analyze the results, we turned to the Matplotlib library.

Tree Leaf Classifier

In a separate project, I designed a classifier based on convolutional networks (ConvNets or CNN) to identify different types of tree leaves. This classifier takes images of leaves as input and is capable of categorizing them with high precision.

The use of convolutional networks for this purpose proved to be a wise choice, as these networks are especially suitable for tasks related to images and visual patterns.

In summary, these projects, carried out within the framework of the Master's in Computer Science at the Politecnico di Milano, reflect the application of advanced artificial intelligence techniques to address complex and practical problems in the field of data science.
