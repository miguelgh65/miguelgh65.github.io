Master's Thesis: Deep Learning Voice Mimic for Alexa

The core of this 2022 project focused on the development of an advanced system to clone voices in Spanish. The need for high-quality voice cloning is becoming increasingly prominent in contemporary applications, and this project aimed to address that demand precisely.

We used "Tacotron" as our primary neural network for the voice synthesis task. Tacotron has proven to be highly effective in generating voice audio that is nearly indistinguishable from an actual human recording. Its ability to convert text to speech, and do so with natural variations and intonations, made it the perfect choice for our project.

In addition to Tacotron, we decided to integrate the GPT-3 API, especially before the existence of ChatGPT. This integration not only allowed for voice cloning but also transformed the system into an automatic speaker. When combined, Tacotron and GPT-3 formed a system that could not only replicate voices in Spanish accurately but also generate coherent and contextually relevant responses and conversations in real-time.

In summary, this project represented a significant advancement in voice synthesis and human-machine interaction, offering potential applications in virtual assistants, automatic response systems, and more.
